# When to Upgrade Your Disc Golf Bag (And What to Buy)

You've outgrown your starter shoulder bag. Your discs are falling out, your back hurts, and you have no room for a jacket or extra water. It's time for a backpack. But which one should you buy?

## Sign #1: You Bag More Than 12 Discs
Once you start carrying different plastics and stabilities of the same mold (like an overstable Teebird and an understable Leopard), a shoulder sling becomes a disorganized mess. Backpacks organize your discs so you can pull what you need instantly.

## Sign #2: Tournament and Travel Needs
A 2-hour casual round is one thing. A 6-hour tournament means you need an umbrella, a folding stool, a jacket, snacks, and a large fluid bladder. Only a backpack provides this structure.
