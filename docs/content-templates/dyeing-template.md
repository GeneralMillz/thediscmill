# How to Dye Disc Golf Discs: A Beginner's Guide

Dyeing your own discs transforms stock plastic into one-of-a-kind art. Whether you want a simple stencil logo or a crazy cosmic burst, the process is incredibly rewarding. Here is the exact starter setup you need to dye your first disc.

## 1. The Right Plastic
**CRITICAL**: You cannot dye base plastics (like Innova DX, Discraft Pro-D, or Dynamic Discs Prime). The dye will simply wash off. You MUST use premium, transparent, or star-like plastics (Innova Star/Champion, Discraft ESP/Z, MVP Neutron/Proton). White discs yield the best colors.

## 2. The Dye Itself
You cannot use standard fabric dye. You need synthetic dyes designed for plastics.

## 4. The Tools
To manipulate the dye into cool patterns, and to clean the disc before you start, you'll need a few cheap household items:

- Ultimate Frisbee (to hold the glue bed)
- Toothpicks or sewing needles (for swirling the dye)
- Acetone & Cotton Balls (to wipe the factory stamp off the disc before dyeing)
  👉 [Check Price on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=100%25+Pure+Acetone&tag=thediscmill-20)

## The Process
1. Wipe the factory stamp off the disc using acetone. Wash it with dish soap.
2. Fill an ultimate frisbee with a 1-inch layer of clear glue.
3. Mix your ProChem dye powder with a little splash of acetone and drop it onto the glue bed.
4. Swirl the colors with a toothpick.
5. Gently lay the disc face-down into the bed.
6. Wait 24 hours. Peel it out, wash it off in the sink, and go throw your masterpiece!
