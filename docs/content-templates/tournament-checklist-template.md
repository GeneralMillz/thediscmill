# The Ultimate PDGA Tournament Checklist

Playing in your first PDGA sanctioned tournament is thrilling, but it's physically and mentally demanding. An amateur tournament typically involves two rounds in a single day, meaning you'll be on the course for 8+ hours. If you aren't prepared, your score will suffer. Here is exactly what you need to pack.

## 1. Portable Seating
Tournament rounds are notoriously slow. A 4-person card on a backed-up course means you'll be standing around waiting to tee off on every single hole. 

- **Top Pick**: Camp Time Roll-A-Stool
- **Why it works**: It’s lightweight, sits high so it's easy to stand up from, and saves your legs during 20-minute backups.

## 2. Weather Mitigation (Rain & Sun)
You have to play through rain, wind, and blistering heat.

- **Top Pick (Rain)**: Disc Golf Bag Rain Fly
- **Top Pick (Sun)**: Sport-Brella or Large Golf Umbrella

## 3. Hand Care & Grip
Sweaty hands in the summer or freezing fingers in the winter will destroy your release point.

- **Top Pick**: Friction Gloves & Whale Sacs
- 👉 [Check Price on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Friction+Disc+Golf+Gloves&tag=thediscmill-20)

## 4. Tournament Hydration & Fuel
You will burn thousands of calories. Water isn't enough; you need electrolytes and easily digestible energy.

- **Top Pick**: Liquid I.V. Hydration Multiplier & Clif Bars
- 👉 [Check Price on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Liquid+IV+Hydration+Multiplier&tag=thediscmill-20)

## 5. PDGA Rules Quick Reference
Don't get hit with penalty strokes because you don't know the rules about out-of-bounds, foot faults, or casual relief. Keep a digital or physical copy of the PDGA rulebook in your bag.
